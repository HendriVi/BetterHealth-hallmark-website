The premise

Insulin resistance, inflammation and disrupted cellular energy quietly narrow the fuel supply the brain depends on.

Metabolic brain health treats those root causes rather than symptoms alone, which is why the assessment begins with measurement, not a questionnaire.

The assessment

We measure the systems standard panels leave out.

A routine blood test was never built to explain mood, focus or mental stamina. We read the metabolic, inflammatory and neural signals the research ties to brain performance, and your numbers become the map every decision is drawn from.

What care looks like

One method, in sequence.

Each step depends on the one before it. We measure, interpret, intervene, then measure again, with a physician holding the plan throughout.

01 / Measure

Read the system

A comprehensive biomarker panel and a quantitative EEG establish how your metabolism, inflammation, hormones and brain rhythm are actually behaving.

02 / Interpret

Find the drivers

A physician reads your results against the evidence to locate the metabolic and inflammatory patterns most likely shaping how you feel.

03 / Intervene

Nutrition as medicine

A prescribed, biomarker-guided nutrition protocol with targeted repletion, and medication managed clinically alongside it.

04 / Optimize

Measure again

We retest, track the movement in your markers and your experience, and adjust the plan. The budget is closed by measurement, not assumption.

The core intervention

Nutrition, prescribed like medicine.

The controlled research is clear enough to act on. A ketogenic metabolic protocol where the biology calls for it, a Mediterranean pattern for many, and targeted omega-3 and micronutrient repletion, each chosen against your measured markers. This is a clinical intervention with dosing, titration and follow-up, always physician-supervised.

Real-world results, backed by science

What a supervised ketogenic protocol produced in a Stanford trial.

Outcomes from the first US clinical trial of ketogenic therapy for serious mental illness since 1965. Twenty-one adults, each carrying a metabolic abnormality, followed a supervised protocol for four months while continuing their medication.

100%reversal of metabolic syndrome among those who met criteria at baseline
~79%clinically meaningful psychiatric improvement on the CGI scale
~10%average reduction in body weight over the four-month protocol
>80%dietary adherence, side effects mostly limited to the first three weeks

Source — Sethi et al. (2024), Psychiatry Research, 335, 115866. Pilot trial. Findings are preliminary and adjunctive to standard care. Individual results vary.
